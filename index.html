The Cost of Freedom - Book Sprint

Call for participation

Nov 2nd to 6th - Marseilles, France

The Cost of Freedom is a book that will be written in Marseilles from November 2nd to 6th. We are looking for activists, artists, designers, developers, researchers, and writers involved with free knowledge movements (free/open access/culture/data/education/government/hardware/science/software/wiki) to join the effort and take part in this Book Sprint.

Throughout history and still today freedom comes with many costs, not least responsibility. Those struggling for freedom bear every kind of cost imaginable: social, psychological, financial, bodily. Often bystanders also bear many of these costs. The best strategy for gaining and protecting freedom, given known and unknowns costs, is almost never unambiguous. Occasionally it isn't clar what freedom means.

Free knowledge movements by contrast seem rather safe and straightforward. Considering the costs borne by millions to obtain, for example freedom from slavery or freedom to vote, it seems dangerous, or perhaps just presumptious, to consider the costs of free-as-in-freedom culture, software, research, etc. But this is what we will attempt to do, and attempt to do so with appropriate humility. For the costs are important: without free knowledge, the future will be unfree. Right?

The costs and choices implied are also interesting, varied, and not shared equally. Many people may use and produce bits of free knowledge, but any atttempt at rigor quickly runs into tremendous barriers, in every field. Members of many groups face discrimination within free knowledge movements, or face unequal barriers to participation due to language and economics. Occasionally work for free knowledge intersects with broader and historic movements for freedom in a very tangible and immediate way, and the costs become unlimited.

During intense 5 days, a group of 10-12 people will gather in Marseilles to discuss how free knowledge movements are built and what are the real costs attached to them. We will work together to see further than the fog of our news feeds and produce some sense by using the Book Sprint methodology created by Adam Hyde.

The Cost of Freedom will be published on Nov 6 and freely available online under CC0.
